# Corpus Observations

This file distills recurring ideas from the post-924 and 2026 source set.

## Main signals

- The corpus repeatedly shifts toward quarterly market outlook and explicit regime framing.
- Policy wording, transmission strength, and liquidity follow-through matter more than isolated narratives.
- A-share discussions become more tied to market-level conditions, risk appetite, and whether the move is structural or tactical.
- The 2026 materials extend the same style of thinking forward: more direct market-strategy language, more explicit scenario framing, and stronger focus on what changes the base case.

## How to use these observations

- Start from regime and policy chain before naming industries.
- Translate titles like market outlook, 3800, 4000, or quarterly strategy into variables and invalidation conditions.
- If the user asks for positioning, respond with scenario-based exposure logic instead of direct personalized instructions.
