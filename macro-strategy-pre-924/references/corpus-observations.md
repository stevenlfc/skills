# Corpus Observations

This file distills recurring ideas from the pre-924 source set.

## Main signals

- Technology is often discussed as strategically important when valuations are already deeply compressed.
- Healthcare cannot be judged in isolation from the broader market regime and policy backdrop.
- Lower-rate expectations matter for allocation logic, but the rate transcript itself is noisy and should be treated cautiously.
- Insurance is framed as a wealth-stage and household balance-sheet topic, not a daily hot-theme trade.
- Quant belongs mainly to the execution layer after the macro and industry thesis is formed.

## How to use these observations

- Prefer repeated cross-file patterns over one transcript sentence.
- Translate observations into policy, liquidity, and industry transmission chains.
- If the user asks for implementation, keep it light unless they explicitly want tactics.
